The Ultimate Guide to Splitting Groceries Without Ruining Friendships

One of the hardest parts of co-living isn't the rent—it's the groceries. Disputes over who bought the milk or who ate the last slice of bread have ruined more flatmate relationships than late-night music. Here is the ultimate guide to managing food expenses.

Method 1: The "Everything is Shared" Approach

This works best when flatmates have similar eating habits. You create a shared kitty (e.g., ₹3000 each per month) and use it exclusively for household groceries. This method promotes bonding but requires massive trust and similar dietary preferences.

Method 2: The "Basics Only" Strategy

A hybrid approach where you only share the absolute essentials: cooking oil, salt, sugar, dish soap, and maybe onions/potatoes. Everything else—snacks, meat, specialty foods—is purchased individually. This is highly recommended for working professionals who eat out often.

Method 3: Completely Separate Kitchens

If one person is vegan and the other is a bodybuilder prepping 6 meals a day, sharing groceries is financially catastrophic. Simply draw boundaries in the fridge (top shelf is yours, bottom is mine) and buy absolutely everything separately.
