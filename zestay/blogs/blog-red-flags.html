5 Red Flags to Watch Out For When House Hunting

House hunting can be an exhausting endeavor, and desperation sometimes leads renters to ignore warning signs. Unfortunately, the real estate market is filled with smooth-talking scammers and properties that look great online but are unlivable in reality. Here are 5 massive red flags you must never ignore.

1. The "Pay a Booking Fee Before Visiting" Scam

If an owner or broker demands a "gate pass fee," "visiting charge," or a token advance via UPI before they have even shown you the property, block them immediately. This is the most common scam targeting desperation. A legitimate owner will never charge you simply to view the property.

2. "Too Good to be True" Pricing

Does the listing feature luxurious, modern interiors in a high-demand area, but the rent is 40% below market rate? It is almost certainly a bait-and-switch. Scammers post stolen images to lure you in, either to extort a booking fee or eventually show you a completely different, much lower quality house.

3. Unreasonable Restrictions

While some ground rules are normal, severe micro-management is a red flag. If the landlord specifies extreme rules like no online food delivery after 9 PM, no socializing, or dictating what you can cook, understand that your life will be heavily restricted. Ask about rules regarding guests and curfews before handing over the deposit.

4. Hidden Maintenance & Society Charges

Always ask: "Is the rent inclusive of maintenance?" Some landlords advertise a highly competitive rent, only to slam you with a heavy monthly society maintenance fee, water bill, or mandatory club membership fee after you have already committed.

5. Fresh Patchwork Paint and Damp Odors

During a physical visit, inspect the walls carefully. If you smell a strong musty odor or see fresh, patchy paint strictly in corners or near windows, it's a masking job for severe dampness and leakage issues. These problems will erupt during the monsoon, ruining your furniture and health.
