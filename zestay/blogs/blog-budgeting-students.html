Living on a Budget: A Student's Guide to Shared Accommodation

Being a student is all about balancing academics, social life, and—most importantly—your finances. When you move into a shared apartment or PG, managing shared costs becomes a major part of your life. Don't worry, budgeting doesn't mean giving up on fun; it just means being smart and proactive. Here is our ultimate student guide to saving money while living with flatmates.

1. Understand Your Total Cost of Living

Rent is only one part of the equation. Always calculate the "all-in" cost, which includes electricity, Wi-Fi, water, society maintenance, and food. Ensure everyone in the house has a clear understanding of how these costs will be split from day one.

2. The Shared Pantry Strategy

Buying groceries individually can lead to a lot of food waste and higher overall costs. Consider a "Shared Pantry" for staples like oil, spices, rice, and cleaning supplies. Group buying often allows for bulk discounts and significantly reduces your monthly supermarket bill.

3. Master the Art of Meal Prepping

Eating out is the biggest budget-killer for students. By planning your meals and cooking in bulk with your flatmates, you can save thousands of rupees every month. Plus, it's a great way to bond after a long day at college!

4. Track Expenses with Technology

Forget the old-school notebook. Use apps like Splitwise to record shared bills instantly. This prevents the "I’ll pay you back later" awkwardness and ensures everyone is on the same page about their financial commitments.

5. Limit Subscription Overlap

Does everyone in the house need a separate Netflix or Amazon Prime account? Probably not. Talk to your flatmates and consider sharing subscription costs for streaming services or Wi-Fi to drastically cut down on recurring monthly expenses.

6. Be Energy Conscious

That AC or high-power heater can blow up your electricity bill if left on unnecessarily. Establish a culture of switching off lights and appliances when not in use. Small habits can lead to significant savings on utility bills over a year.
