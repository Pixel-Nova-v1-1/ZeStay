7 Essential Safety Checks Before Moving Into a Shared Flat

Moving into a new city and finding a shared flat is an exciting milestone. However, in the rush to secure a place, safety often takes a backseat. At ZeStay, we prioritize your security above all else. Before you sign that agreement or pay the deposit, make sure you've covered these essential safety checks.

1. Verify Your Flatmates' Identity

You'll be sharing your most personal space with these individuals. Don't hesitate to ask for a valid ID or professional link (like LinkedIn). Knowing who you live with is the first step toward a safe home environment.

2. Check the Locks and Windows

Inspect every entrance. Are the locks sturdy? Do the windows close securely? If you're on a lower floor, ensure there are safety grills. Don't be afraid to ask the landlord to replace faulty locks before you move in.

3. Fire Safety Equipment

Does the building have fire extinguishers or a functional fire alarm system? Check for clear exit paths. In high-rise apartments, understanding the evacuation plan is crucial.

4. Shared Space Security

Is there a security guard at the gate? Are there CCTV cameras in common areas like the lobby or parking lot? A well-guarded society significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized entries.

5. Background Check the Landlord

A "landlord" might just be a sub-renter pretending to own the place. Always verify property ownership documents (like the Index II or Electricity Bill) to ensure you're dealing with the rightful owner.

6. Review the Rental Agreement Thoroughly

Ensure all verbal promises are in writing. Look for clauses regarding notice periods, deposit refunds, and maintenance responsibilities. A clear agreement prevents future legal and financial disputes.

7. Trust Your Instincts

If something feels 'off' during your visit—whether it's the behavior of potential flatmates or the vibe of the neighborhood—don't ignore it. Your gut feeling is a powerful safety tool.
