The Ultimate Flatmate Agreement Checklist

Moving in with a new flatmate is an exciting milestone. It means shared responsibilities, split rent, and potentially, a new friend for life. However, living with someone new also comes with its own set of challenges. To ensure a harmonious living environment, relying strictly on verbal promises is rarely enough. Having a clear, agreed-upon framework from day one can prevent countless arguments down the road.

1. Financial Responsibilities

Money is often the most sensitive topic among flatmates. Being transparent upfront saves a lot of headaches:

  • Rent and Deposit: Who transfers the rent to the landlord? When is the deadline? How is the deposit split?
  • Utility Bills: Establish a fair system for splitting electricity, Wi-Fi, water, and gas. What happens if one person runs the AC 24/7?
  • Shared Expenses: Determine how you will handle common household items like cleaning supplies, cooking oil, and the maid's salary. App usage like Splitwise can be extremely helpful here.

2. Cleaning and Chores

Differing standards of cleanliness can ruin even the best flatmate relationships. Establish ground rules early:

  • Will you hire a domestic helper, or split the chores?
  • If splitting chores, create a visible roster for taking out the trash, cleaning the bathroom, and wiping down the kitchen counters.
  • Set expectations for doing dishes. (e.g., "Wash your own dishes within 24 hours").

3. Guests and Privacy

Your flat is a shared sanctuary. Respecting each other's space is paramount.

  • Overnight Guests: How many nights a week is acceptable for a partner or friend to stay over? Should permission be asked first?
  • Parties and Gatherings: Establish "quiet hours" (e.g., no loud music after 11 PM on weekdays). Discuss whether throwing parties requires unanimous consent.
  • Shared Spaces vs Private Spaces: Clarify boundaries. For example, knocking before entering a bedroom is a basic but essential rule.

4. Conflict Resolution

Disagreements are inevitable, but how you handle them makes all the difference. Agree to communicate openly rather than leaving passive-aggressive sticky notes. Schedule a brief monthly "flat meeting" over pizza to discuss any underlying grievances before they escalate.
