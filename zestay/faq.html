Frequently Asked Questions

General Queries & Getting Started

Is Zestay completely free to use?

Yes, Zestay is currently 100% free for all individual users. You can sign up, complete your profile, browse verified property listings, take the compatibility quiz, and securely match and chat with potential flatmates without paying any subscription fees or hidden brokerage charges.

Can I search for flatmates without already having a flat?

Absolutely! We designed Zestay to be highly flexible. If you don't have a place yet, simply select "Looking for a Flatmate" during sign-up. You can browse through other users in the same situation and use our “Team-Up” mode. You and your highly matched future flatmate can then search for a whole flat together.

How does Zestay protect my privacy during the search?

Your privacy is our top priority. We mask your personal contact details (phone number and email address) from the public directory. Communication is securely facilitated through our encrypted in-app messaging system. You only share your phone number when you decide you completely trust the potential landlord or flatmate.

Matching Algorithm & Verification

How exactly is my "Match Score" calculated?

Our proprietary matching algorithm analyzes the critical lifestyle datapoints gathered from your "Vibe Check" onboarding quiz. We deeply compare vital compatibility factors including cleanliness standards, guest preferences, dietary habits (vegetarian vs non-vegetarian), sleep schedules, and smoking limits. A score above 80% usually indicates excellent harmony and a drastically lower chance of roommate disputes.

Why is OTP mobile verification mandatory?

To eliminate scammers and fake brokerage profiles, Zestay enforces a strict OTP mobile verification on all accounts. This builds a trustworthy ecosystem where you can be fully confident that the individual you are chatting with is a genuine tenant or a real property owner.

What if I made a mistake on my lifestyle questionnaire?

Preferences change! You can dynamically update your Vibe Check answers at any time by navigating to your Dashboard > Profile Settings. Once saved, our engine will automatically recalibrate your match percentages against other users in the network.

Listings & Landlord Information

How long does a property listing stay active?

To prevent the site from cluttering with dead leads, all listings automatically expire after 30 days. However, as an owner, you will receive a notification prompting you to renew the listing with one click if the property is still unrented. If you find a tenant early, you can instantly mark the listing as "Occupied".

Are PG (Paying Guest) accommodations and independent flats separated?

Yes. We understand that finding a PG has very different requirements than renting an independent apartment. Our intuitive UI filters clearly separate single occupancy rooms, shared PG dorms, and fully independent 1BHK/2BHK flats so you never waste time navigating irrelevant inventory.

Can brokers post listings on Zestay?

Zestay is primarily built for direct, peer-to-peer housing connections (Owner to Tenant or Flatmate to Flatmate) to eliminate excessive brokerage fees. If a user is identified as a commercial broker posing as an independent owner, their account will be permanently banned to protect our community's financial interests.

Payments & Legalities

Does Zestay handle rent payments or security deposits?

No. Zestay operates strictly as a connection and discovery platform. We do not process rent, nor do we hold security deposits in escrow. All financial transactions must be handled securely off-platform directly between you and the verified landlord. Always demand a legal receipt when paying a deposit.

Who is responsible for drafting the rental agreement?

The drafting and registration of the legal Rent Agreement (Leave and License Agreement) is the mutual responsibility of the property owner and the tenant. We strongly recommend registering your agreement formally with the sub-registrar office to ensure absolute legal protection for both parties under Indian property laws.
